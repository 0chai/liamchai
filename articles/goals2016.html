Review of 2016 Goals

Below are my reflections from the goal setting session I organised last year. We set 1 year, 10 year and lifetime goals. I set very specific and in hindsight, very ambitious goals. Nonetheless the exercise was valuable and I hope to have it as a yearly ritual with friends and family.

Reflections from Goals in 2016 

The recommendation in the goal setting exercise was to set very specific and measurable goals. Without measurement, it would be difficult to get feedback, and without feedback deliberate practice is almost impossible.

I’ve added percentages below of how I fared with each goal. When I think of percentages, school grades come to mind. Passing tests, failing tests. My goals list is not an exam, it’s more a mirror board to help me self-reflect and make tweaks in my life where necessary.

(RED = <50% ORANGE = 50% GREEN = >66% )

More reflections

Last year was the first time I set goals with strong intentions behind them. I printed the list off, laminated it and stuck it on my wall. In hindsight laminating wasn’t necessary because the goals changed quite a bit. My goals for 2016 were overly ambitious. However on reflection it also shined a light on how much time I do waste and the misalignment with what I want in life and what I actually spend time doing in life. I notice that misalignment is getting smaller, but it’s still there

Some Learnings:

  • Time capital is limited. The Time You Have (in Jellybeans) video highlights that very well. Therefore with limited time, it’s impossible to go after every single desire. So to reflect on desires ano imbue them with some wisdom makes sense. What is it that I genuinely want? What is my heart’s desire?
  • Read my goals everyday. Clarifying and writing them out is the easy part. Remembering them when the wave of life sets in is the difficult part.
  • There’s a balance between sharing your goals with everyone and keeping them to yourself. The former might give you a false satisfaction and sense of approval from people. The latter means you might lose out on the synchronicities and network power of sharing your goals.

Themes for 2017: SIMPLICITY, FLEXIBILITY, SILENCE.
